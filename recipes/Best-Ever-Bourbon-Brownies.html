Best-Ever Bourbon Brownies

General Information

Prep: 35 min

Bake: 25 min at 350 F

Servings: 16 brownies

Ingredients

    For the brownies

  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup butter
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans, toasted (explained below)
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons bourbon
  • 2 ounces semisweet chocolate, melted Pecan halves, toasted (optional)

    • For the frosting

  • 3 tablespoons softened butter
  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon bourbon or milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla
  • *additional teaspoons of bourbon or milk* (explained below)

Bakeware

    Keep your hands out of the cookie jar!

  • elextric mixer
  • set of measuring cups
  • set of measuring spoons
  • wire rack
  • wooden Spoon
  • 1 medium bowl
  • 1 medium saucepan
  • aluminum foil
  • 8x8x2-inch baking pan
  • brush

How to Make the Best-Ever Bourbon Brownies

Overview

Below is a general walkthrough of the entire recipe.

  1. Prep the utencils and oven.
  2. Toast the pecans.
  3. Make the brownie batter.
  4. Bake the batter.
  5. Make the frosting.
  6. Let the brownies cool and frost them.
  7. Bon Appetit!

Toasting the Pecans

    Toasting nuts of any kind adds flavor and fragrance compared to raw nuts

  1. Toasting via Oven

    1. Spread some nuts in a shallow baking pan

    2. Bake at 350 F between 5 to 10 miinutes. During this time, shake the pan once or twice.

    3. Check the nuts to avoid burning them.

  2. Toasting via Microwave

    1. Spread some nuts in a shallow baking pan.

    2. Cook them on high in 1 minute intervals.

    3. Routinely check to avoid burning the nuts. Total number of 1 minute intervals depends on the type of nut.

Step by Step

    1. For the batter

    1. Preheat the oven to 350 F. Line the 8x8x2-inch baking pan with the aluminum foil. The foil should extend one inch over the edge of the pan. Grease the foil and set the pan elsewhere. In the medium saucepan, mix together the sugar, water and the butter. Cook this combination on medium heat until just boiling. Afterward, remove from the heat and set aside.

    2. Add the chocolate pieces and stir until melted. Beat in the eggs and vanilla with the wooden spoon. Add and stir the baking soda, salt and flour. Add and stir in the chopped pecans.

    3. Pour the batter into the prepped pan and spread evenly.

    4. Bake for around 25 minutes or if a wooden toothpick inserted at the center comes out clean. Check to make sure the edges start to peal away from the sides of the pan.

    5. Grab your wirerack and place the finished pan utop. Brush the brownies with bourbon and set aside to cool.

    6. Frost the brownies once cool and cut them into bars. Place some of the melted chocolate with a pecan on top, if wanted.

    2. For the frosting

    1. Grab your medium bowl and electric mixer.

    2. Beat in 3 tablespoons of softened butter on a medium to high speed for 30 seconds.

    3. Gradually add the powdered sugar. Beat the mixture well.

    4. Beat in the bourbon and the vanilla

    5. Beat in additional milk or bourbon to desired consistency.

Storage

These brownies should last up to three days. I reccomend placing them in a single layer of an airtight container. Keep it at room temperature.
